Learn the fundamentals of web design to create your personal blog or business site yourself.

Do you:

  • own a business and want to fully manage your own website
  • want to start a new hobby or blog
  • have content you want to publish online
  • want to add web design to your list of marketable skills

This seven (7) part series of workshops will be a web design fundamentals course focusing on HTML and CSS and will compliment your self-study/practice. You’ll learn new skills, discover free resources, ask questions, work on your projects, and create community. We will meet Thursdays from 6:30pm to 8:30pm. Interested, but this day/time does not work for you? Please tell us your availability.

Satisfaction Guarantee: We are confident that you will be pleased with the Intro to Web Design Series; however, if at the end you are not completely satisfied, we offer you a nearly-full refund of $285 (less 5% processing fee).

You will learn:

Part 1 – Introduction to the Web and Website Structure and Style

Thursday, July 7, 2016  @  6:30pm - 8:30pm

  • What is the web? How do websites work?
  • Introduction to the structure of a website: HTML
  • Introduction to the style of a website: CSS
  • Folders (directories), files, and absolute and relative links

Part 2 – Semantics and Selectors

Thursday, July 14, 2016  @  6:30pm - 8:30pm

  • HTML and semantics (Writing Descriptive HTML)
  • CSS specificity (More on CSS Declarations)

Part 3 – Getting Setup: Domain, Hosting, FTP, and Personalized Email

Thursday, July 21, 2016  @  6:30pm - 8:30pm

  • Domain registration
  • Hosting options
  • Transferring files to your web host
  • Establishing a custom email (yourname @ yourdomain.com) for FREE that you can access through gmail or other email client

Part 4 – The Box Model and Positioning

Thursday, July 28, 2016  @  6:30pm - 8:30pm

  • Learn how the box model and the position and float properties influence how content is displayed (more-in-depth CSS)

Part 5 – Not-from-Scratch HTML/CSS and Content Management with Blogging Platforms

Thursday, August 4, 2016  @  6:30pm - 8:30pm

  • Front-end templates and CSS Frameworks
  • Free content resources to add to your site: fonts and images
  • A brief overview of blogging platform options, including Wordpress, Tumblr, and Jekyll (This class does not teach how to use these platforms.)

Part 6 – Integrating Paypal, Social Media, and Google Analytics

Thursday, August 11, 2016  @  6:30pm - 8:30pm

  • Add Paypal buy now buttons
  • Links to facebook, instagram, twitter, etc.
  • Display social posts on your site
  • Integrating Google Analytics with your site

Part 7 – Putting It All Together

Thursday, August 18, 2016  @  6:30pm - 8:30pm

  • Final overview of what we have learned and where you can go next
  • Complete any unfinished topics and answer final questions
  • Review of resources and next steps to complete your project
