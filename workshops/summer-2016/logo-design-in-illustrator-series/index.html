Create your personal or business logo and learn how to use the vector graphics editor Adobe Illustrator.

Are you:

  • an artist, writer, or small business owner who wants full creative freedom to design his or her logo
  • needing to translate an existing drawn logo into a digital file
  • wanting to create a logo, but don’t know where to start
  • interested in learning about how to use Adobe Illustrator to create vector images
  • wanting to add Adobe Illustrator to your list of marketable skills

In this three (3) part series of workshops, you will learn how to use Adobe Illustrator to create your personal or business logo. Please note that you will need to do some preparation outside of the workshop time to create your logo in part two.

You will need access to Adobe Illustrator for these workshops. You can download a free trial here.

We will meet for three Saturdays from 1pm to 3pm. Interested, but this day/time does not work for you? Please tell us your availability.

Satisfaction Guarantee: We are confident that you will be pleased with the Logo Design in Illustrator Series; however, if at the end you are not completely satisfied, we offer you a nearly-full refund of $114 (less 5% processing fee).

You will learn:

Part 1 – Introduction to Illustrator Tools and Logo Creation Guide

Saturday, July 23, 2016  @  1pm - 3pm

  • What is a vector image
  • The selection, direct selection, pen, type, and various shape tools
  • Introduction to working with paths and anchor points
  • Working with artboards and layers
  • Modifying stroke type, width, and weight
  • Aligning and grouping objects
  • Learn about free resources to use in developing your logo such as free-for-commercial-use fonts
  • Logo best practices – how to create a memorable and succinct image that describes your business and translates well from small (e.g. business card) to large (e.g.billboard) applications.

Part 2 – Craft Your Logo (By Hand/Digitally)

Saturday, July 30, 2016  @  1pm - 3pm

  • Combine type and image to create several logo options
  • How to bring your image(s) into Illustrator
  • Continued exploration of paths: joining, pathfinder methods, and compound paths
  • Various transformation actions

Part 3 – Review Logos and Preparing Final Logo Files

Saturday, August 6, 2016  @  1pm - 3pm

  • Expand and expand appearance
  • Creating text outlines
  • Exporting your logo in different formats
  • Answer final questions
